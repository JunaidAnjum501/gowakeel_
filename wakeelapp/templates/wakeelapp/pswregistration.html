Understanding Pakistan Single Window (PSW)

Pakistan Single Window (PSW) is a transformative initiative by the Government of Pakistan designed to streamline cross-border trade processes. It serves as a centralized digital platform where traders can submit standardized information to fulfill all regulatory requirements related to imports, exports, and transit.

Before PSW, businesses had to navigate through multiple government agencies, submit redundant information, and deal with complex paper-based processes. PSW eliminates these inefficiencies by providing a single point of submission for all trade-related documentation and approvals.

By registering with PSW, businesses can significantly reduce clearance times, lower compliance costs, and minimize the risk of errors or delays in their international trade operations.

Documents Required for PSW Registration

To get started with your PSW registration, you'll need to prepare the following documents:

Personal Identification

  • Valid CNIC (Computerized National Identity Card) - front and back copies
  • Registered mobile number (linked with CNIC for OTP verification)
  • Active personal email address (not shared with other PSW accounts)

Business Documentation

  • Business registration documents (SECP certificate for companies, partnership deed, or sole proprietorship registration)
  • Company letterhead (for authorization letters and declarations)
  • Memorandum and Articles of Association (for companies)

Tax Documentation

  • Active FBR NTN (National Tax Number) certificate
  • IRIS credentials (username and password for FBR portal)
  • Sales Tax Registration certificate (if applicable)
  • Income Tax Return (latest filed)

Additional Requirements

  • Authorization letter (if registering on behalf of someone else)
  • Digital photograph (passport size with white background)
  • Specimen signature (scanned copy on white paper)
  • Bank account details (for payment of registration fees)

Document Preparation Tips

  • All documents should be clear, legible scans (300dpi recommended)
  • File sizes should not exceed 2MB per document
  • Acceptable formats: PDF, JPG, PNG (PDF preferred for multi-page documents)
  • Ensure all documents are valid and not expired
