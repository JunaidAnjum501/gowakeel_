Company Registration in Pakistan: FBR vs SECP

Company registration in Pakistan is monitored by both the FBR (Federal Board of Revenue) and the SECP (Securities and Exchange Commission of Pakistan).

If you're operating as a sole proprietor, registration is done only with the FBR, which makes it simpler and easier to manage. However, a Private Limited Company or Single Member Company (SMC) must be registered with both FBR and SECP, and must adhere to all compliance requirements under the Companies Act and Company Ordinance.

This structure provides credibility, transparency, and legal protection, but also comes with annual filing and audit obligations.

Types of Company Registration in Pakistan

A business owned and operated by a single individual. Simple registration process with FBR only. The owner has complete control but also bears all liabilities personally.

Advantages

  • Simple and inexpensive to establish
  • Complete control over business decisions
  • Minimal regulatory requirements
  • No separate tax filing (personal income tax only)
  • Easy to dissolve or transfer ownership

Limitations

  • Unlimited personal liability for business debts
  • Limited access to capital and financing
  • Business existence tied to owner's life
  • Less credibility with clients and vendors
  • Limited ability to work with international platforms

A Private Limited Company with only one director/shareholder. Provides limited liability protection while allowing sole ownership. Must be registered with both FBR and SECP.

Advantages

  • Limited liability protection for personal assets
  • Enhanced credibility with clients and partners
  • Perpetual existence independent of owner
  • Easier access to business loans and capital
  • Ability to work with international platforms

Limitations

  • Higher setup and maintenance costs
  • More regulatory compliance requirements
  • Annual filing and audit obligations
  • More complex tax filing requirements
  • Requires maintaining proper corporate records

Key Requirements

  • Minimum paid-up capital of PKR 100,000
  • One director who is also the sole shareholder
  • Registered office address in Pakistan
  • Memorandum & Articles of Association
  • Digital signature from NIFT
  • Annual returns and financial statements

A business entity with multiple shareholders (minimum 2, maximum 50) with limited liability. Shares cannot be offered to the general public. Provides a formal structure with clear separation between company and personal assets.

Advantages

  • Limited liability protection for all shareholders
  • Enhanced business credibility and professional image
  • Easier to raise capital through multiple shareholders
  • Perpetual existence independent of shareholders
  • Transferable ownership through share transfers
  • Tax benefits and deductions not available to individuals

Limitations

  • More complex and costly to establish than sole proprietorship
  • Extensive regulatory compliance requirements
  • Mandatory annual audits and financial reporting
  • Cannot raise funds from the general public
  • Decision-making may require shareholder consensus
  • Double taxation in some cases (corporate and dividend tax)

Key Requirements

  • Minimum 2 shareholders, maximum 50
  • At least 2 directors (can be shareholders)
  • Minimum paid-up capital of PKR 100,000
  • Registered office address in Pakistan
  • Memorandum & Articles of Association
  • Annual returns and audited financial statements
  • Company secretary appointment
  • Annual general meetings

A business arrangement where two or more individuals share ownership and responsibilities. Partners share profits according to the partnership deed and are jointly liable for business debts.

Advantages

  • Easy and inexpensive to establish
  • Shared financial investment and risk
  • Combined knowledge, skills and resources
  • Flexible management structure
  • Less regulatory compliance than companies
  • Tax advantages (pass-through taxation)

Limitations

  • Unlimited liability for all partners
  • Each partner liable for actions of other partners
  • Potential for conflicts between partners
  • Limited life (dissolves if partner leaves)
  • Difficult to raise large amounts of capital
  • Less credibility than incorporated entities

Key Requirements

  • Partnership deed/agreement
  • NTN registration with FBR
  • Bank account in partnership name
  • CNIC copies of all partners
  • Business address proof
  • Annual tax returns

Limited Liability Company structure that combines elements of partnership and corporation. Provides liability protection while allowing flexible management structure and tax benefits.

Advantages

  • Limited liability protection for all members
  • Flexible management structure
  • Pass-through taxation (no double taxation)
  • Less formalities than a corporation
  • Easier to attract investors than partnerships
  • No restriction on number of members

Limitations

  • More complex to set up than sole proprietorship
  • Self-employment taxes for managing members
  • Requires more record-keeping than partnerships
  • Ownership transfer restrictions
  • Limited ability to raise capital compared to corporations

Key Requirements

  • Minimum 2 designated partners
  • LLP Agreement
  • Registered office address in Pakistan
  • Name approval from SECP
  • Statement of partners and registered office
  • Annual statement of accounts and solvency

The typical timeframe for company registration in Pakistan is 7-15 business days, depending on the company type and documentation readiness. With Go Wakeel's expedited service, we can complete the process in as little as 7-10 business days for most company types.

For a Private Limited Company or Single Member Company, the minimum paid-up capital requirement is PKR 100,000. However, you can start with a lower authorized capital and increase it later as your business grows. For certain specialized businesses like insurance or banking, higher capital requirements apply.

Yes, foreigners can register a company in Pakistan. They can own up to 100% of the shares in most business sectors. However, there are some additional requirements, including security clearance from relevant authorities and authentication of documents from the Pakistani embassy in their home country. Our team can guide foreign investors through this process.

After registration, companies must comply with several ongoing requirements, including:

  • Filing annual returns with SECP
  • Submitting audited financial statements
  • Filing monthly/quarterly/annual tax returns with FBR
  • Holding annual general meetings
  • Maintaining statutory registers and records
  • Reporting any changes in company structure or directors

Go Wakeel offers comprehensive compliance services to help you meet these requirements.

NTN (National Tax Number) is issued by FBR for income tax purposes and is mandatory for all businesses. STRN (Sales Tax Registration Number) is required only if your business falls under the sales tax regime and has an annual turnover exceeding the threshold set by FBR. Our registration packages include both NTN and STRN registration where applicable.
